Who is Jeffrey Toobin?

Jeffrey Toobin is an American lawyer, author, and pundit. Currently, he serves as a legal analyst for CNN and The New Yorker.

Furthermore, he has also written a book called O.J. Simpson murder case which was also adapted into The People v. O.J. Simpson: American Crime Story and won several Emmy awards.

Who are the parents of Jeffrey Toobin?

Jeffrey was born on May 21, 1960, in New York City, New York, United States. He is the son of Marlene Sanders and Jerome Toobin.

Moreover, his mother was ABC News and CBS News correspondent, and his father was a news broadcasting producer.

His nationality is American and belongs to the Jewish ethnicity.

Education

Initially, the legendary lawyer attended Columbia Grammar and Preparatory School. Later, he completed his graduation with a Bachelor of Arts degree in classics.

In 1986, Jeffrey Toobin graduated from Harvard Law School magna cum laude with a J.D.

Jeffrey Toobin: Professional life, Career

Jeffrey Toobin began his career as a law clerk to a federal judge. Soon after that, he worked as an associate counsel to Independent Counsel Lawrence Edward Walsh during the Iran-Contra affair and Oliver North’s criminal trial.

Furthermore, he also served as an Assistant U.S. Attorney in Brooklyn. However, after three years in charge, he resigned from the U.S. Attorney’s office in Brooklyn.

In 1993, he joined The New Yorker, and just after one year, he became a television legal analyst for ABC 1994.

Furthermore, he has also written a book called O.J. Simpson murder case which was also adapted into The People v. O.J. Simpson: American Crime Story.

His other notable work includes an analysis of Michael Jackson’s 2005 child molestation trial. Additionally, Jeffrey joined CNN in 2002 and conduct his first interview with Martha Stewart about the insider trading charges against her.

Moreover, Jeffrey Toobin was also a member of the private discussion group JournoList.

Nominations, Awards

So far, Jeffrey has won awards from the Columbia Graduate School of Journalism and the Nieman Foundation for Journalism at Harvard University.
