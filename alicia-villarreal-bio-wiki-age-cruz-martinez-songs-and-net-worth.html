Alicia Villarreal Biography

Alicia Villarreal is a Mexican singer and songwriter who was born on 31 August 1971 in Monterrey, Mexico. She took part in different bands in Monterrey, before becoming the lead singer for Grupo Límite in 1995. After nearly eight years of success, Alicia abandoned the band to pursue a solo career.

Alicia Singer

Alicia first performed the song Mundo De Juguetes at Pipo, a local TV show her parents took her to and her first professional performance was at a church fundraiser. Grupo Límite, with her as lead singer, began touring with the more famous Grupo Bronco in 1995. Among their songs were Te Aprovechas, Acariciame, and Solo Contigo. Grupo Limite released six albums altogether, including a live CD recorded in Mexico City.

Alicia released her first solo album in the ranchera style known as Soy Lo Prohibido in 2001 which featured the hit single Te Quedo Grande La Yegua. After the success of the album, she stated she felt conflicted about how to go on with her career due to changing genres. After separating from her bandmates in 2004, she released her second solo album known as Cuando el Corazón se Cruza. The album was produced by her current husband, Cruz Martínez, and they wrote a number of songs together. The album has earned her several awards

Alicia performed for the highly televised Selena Vive, in 2006, where she performed Si Una Vez. She later released this song along with 2 other new songs Punalada Trapera and Ya No Hay Amor on a dual disc of the album Cuando El Corazón Se Cruza. The dual disc also contained 5 remixes to her previous songs and a collection of all her music videos. In 2005, the dual disc was released in Mexico

Alicia released her third solo album titled Orgullo De Mujer with the lead single Insensible a Ti in 2006. The single was the theme song for the telenovela Duelo de Pasiones, on which she made her acting debut, in the role of Raquel. Orgullo de Mujer received a Grammy nomination for Best Mexican, Mexican-American Album.

After nearly a four-year hiatus, Alicia reported she was in the studio recording her 4th solo album entitled La Jefa which was released 23 June 2009, with Caso Perdido, as the lead single. The album debuted at number one on Billboard’s Regional Mexican Albums and overall at number four on its Latin Albums chart. La Jefa obtained mixed reviews upon its release. She released her fifth studio album, La Villarreal on 8 September 2017,
