Blueface Grew Up With an Interest in Basketball and Music

Born in Los Angeles, California on the 20th of January 1997, Blueface grew up in central Los Angeles Mid-City. As a child, he attended several elementary schools before finally moving and settling in with his mother in Santa Clarita Valley. Not long after, he had to move again to go live with his father in Oakland. He finally moved to San Fernando Valley where he attended high school. He schooled at Arleta High School where his interest in music and sports blossomed.

While he was a student at Arleta High School, Blueface joined the football team where he played as the starting quarterback in 2014. His height of 6’4” stood him out in the game and he did exceptionally well. In 2014, he led the team to an East Valley League championship. Blueface began to take an interest in rap music at a young age this made him join the marching band where he played alto saxophone in high school. He listened and looked up to rappers like Snoop Dogg, 50 Cent, and The Game.

After high school, Blueface’s talents didn’t go unnoticed, he committed himself to play for Fayetteville State University. Even though he left in 2016, he briefly played college football. In reference to his experience as a football player. His video of the remix of his song “Thotiana” was a reflection of his past where he portrays a high school quarterback player who was full of prospects but was very disrespectful to his coach because he wants to pursue fame and women.
