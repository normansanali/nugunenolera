Cason Crane On Race To Survive Alaska

USA Network’s brand-new show Race to Survive Alaska hit the screen on April 3, 2023, and it saw eight teams including Cason and Bella battling it out in the Alaskan wilderness for a chance to win $500,000. These teams of two participated in six intense races over 40 days without planes, trains, and automobiles to help them get around. Infact, they had to endure more than 100 miles of inhospitable, previously unexplored terrain on the strength of their own two feet with nothing but only what they can carry.

So, this meant Cason had to utilize his primitive survival skills to source his own food, water, and shelter. Moreover, being on the dangerous Alaskan coastline, marked by its sprawling mountain ranges, ancient glaciers, suffocating rainforests, and threatening wildlife, certainly didn’t make things easier.

Joining Cason and Bella for the inaugural season of Race to Survive Alaska were their co-stars Jeff and Hunter Leininger (father-son duo), Robin Moore and Elizabeth Killham (friends and adventurers), Brett Gattenand Esther Sanderlin (Alaskan hunters), Wilson and Oliver Hoogendorn (siblings), and Max Djenohan and Christian Junkar (reality stars and friends).

How Much Is Cason Crane Net Worth?

Cason Crane flaunted a net worth of above $750 thousand in 2023.

He is an adventurer and the founder of Explorer Cold Brew (the world’s first premium cold brew that lets you choose your caffeine level) and The Rainbow Summits Project.

Through The Rainbow Summits Project, Cason became the fifth youngest and first openly LGBT person to climb Mt. Everest and the Seven Summits (the highest mountain on each continent). He even raised $135,000 for youth suicide prevention and crisis intervention thanks to this feat.

After making a name for himself, Cason then joined Princeton University and graduated four years later with a degree in History and certificates in American Studies and Near Eastern Studies in 2017. Since graduation, Cason has continued to satisfy his craving for exploration and won several prize-winning finishes in ultramarathon and obstacle course races. Also, he has completed half a dozen IRONMAN triathlon races in addition to dozens of marathons, open-water swims, hikes, climbs, and more.

Whilst continuing his adventure, Cason has also worked as a Consultant for Bain & Company, Director of Strategy for Community Bucket, Investment Team Summer Analyst for Oasis500, Finance Intern for the Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee, Documentarian Page for U.S. House of Representatives, and more.
