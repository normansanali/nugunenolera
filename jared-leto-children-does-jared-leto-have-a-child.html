Jared Leto biography

Constance Leto, the mother of Leto, gave birth to him in Bossier City, Louisiana, on December 26, 1971. His mother is of Cajun descent.

His stepfather’s last name is Leto. He and his older brother, Shannon, lived with their mother and his maternal grandparents, Ruby and William Lee Metrejon after his parents split when they were young. When Jared was eight years old, his father committed suicide after getting remarried.

Leto repeatedly relocated from Louisiana to several places across the nation with his family. Leto has said that travelling around a lot was a typical way of life because his mother’s father was an Air Force officer. Leto’s father’s second marriage gave birth to two younger half-brothers for him.

After briefly leaving school in the tenth grade, Leto made the decision to go back and concentrate on his studies at the exclusive Emerson Preparatory School in Washington, D.C. He studied at Philadelphia’s University of the Arts because he was interested in large-scale visual art.

He changed schools to enrol at the School of Visual Arts in New York City after discovering a passion for filmmaking. He wrote and starred in his own short film, Crying Joy, while he was a student there. He also went to the George Washington University’s Corcoran School of the Arts and Design, which is now a part of it.

Leto relocated to Los Angeles in 1992 to seek a career in directing with the intention of performing in supporting roles.

Although he had modest appearances on television, his big break came in 1994 when he was hired as Claire Danes’ love interest Jordan Catalano in the ABC adolescent drama My So-Called Life. The show was short-lived but received positive reviews.

The show received plaudits for how well it captured adolescence, and although being canceled after one season, it developed a devoted cult following. In the same year, Leto appeared in his first television movie, Cool and the Crazy, costarring Alicia Silverstone. He then received his first film part, in the 1995 drama How to Make an American Quilt.
