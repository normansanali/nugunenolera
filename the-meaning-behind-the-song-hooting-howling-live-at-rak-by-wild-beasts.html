The Meaning Behind The Song: Hooting &amp; Howling (Live at RAK) by Wild Beasts

July 18, 2024
blog

As a music critic, I always find myself intrigued by songs that have a deeper meaning hidden beneath their captivating melodies. One such song that has always resonated with me is “Hooting & Howling” by Wild Beasts. I remember stumbling upon this song at a friend’s house and being captivated when I heard it for the first time. The raw energy and thought-provoking lyrics instantly drew me in.

“Carry me, hooting and howling, to the river to wash off my hands, of the hot blood, the sweat and the sand,” the song opens with these lines, painting a vivid imagery of release and cleansing. It suggests a desire to rid oneself of the past, particularly the violence and chaos that may accompany it. The act of hooting and howling serves as a cathartic expression, a way to let go of pent-up emotions.

The following verse delves deeper into the complexities of human nature. “A crude art, a bovver boot ballet – equally elegant and ugly,” sings Wild Beasts. It highlights the contradictions that exist within us all. We can be both graceful and ugly, both thrilled and appalled by our actions. The mention of fisticuffing waltz adds a layer of intensity to the lyrics, suggesting a dance with violence and conflict.

The chorus repeats the phrase “Hooting and howling” several times, almost like a mantra. It serves as a release, a primal expression of emotions. The repetitive nature of the chorus emphasizes the need to let go and embrace our primal instincts in order to find relief and liberation.

In the final verse, Wild Beasts sing, “Any rival who goes for our girls will be left thumb-sucking in terror and bereft of all coffin bearers.” Here, the focus shifts to themes of protection and loyalty, but with a sense of defiance. The imagery implies that anyone who dares to challenge their loved ones will face the consequences, left terrified and alone. It is a stand against those who seek to harm or disrupt their community.

The live version of “Hooting & Howling” performed at RAK Studio gives the song an added intensity. The energy of the band is palpable as they deliver the emotionally charged lyrics. The live rendition brings the song to life in a way that a studio recording simply cannot capture.

In conclusion, “Hooting & Howling” by Wild Beasts is a dynamic and powerful song that explores themes of release, human nature, and loyalty. Its evocative lyrics and mesmerizing melodies make it a standout track. So, the next time you listen to this song, allow yourself to be carried away by its raw energy and embrace the meaning that lies beneath the surface.
